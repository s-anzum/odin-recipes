French Fries

It's as simple as it looks!

French fries are one of the simplest and most universally loved dishes. They require only a few ingredients. Let's make some fresh, crispy fries today!

Ingredients:

(For 8 servings)

  • 2 and half pounds peeled potatoes
  • 1 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 cup regular flour
  • 1 teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1 teaspoon onion salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Half cup water, or as needed
  • Fresh parsley and ketchup (optional)

Steps:

  1. Slice potatoes into French fries. Put them into a bowl of cold water to retain their color.
  2. Heat oil in a saucepan to 350°F (175°C).
  3. Sift flour, garlic salt, onion salt, paprika, and salt into a bowl while the oil is heating.
  4. Stir the mix in water gradually to make a slightly thick batter.
  5. Drain fries and pat dry, dip into batter one at a time.
  6. Lower fries carefully into the hot oil in batches to prevent them from clumping together, fry until golden brown and crispy on all sides, 7 to 10 minutes.
  7. Drain fries on a paper towel-lined plate.
  8. Garnish some parsley, serve hot with ketchup for best experience.
